Pull-Apart Sour Cream and Chive Rolls

Easy pull-apart buns that are baked in a tray using a tangzhong - usually used in Japanese milk bread. They are moist, soft and delicious.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup + 2 tbsp whole milk
  • 625 + 42 g bread flour
  • 1.5 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 50 g sugar
  • 4 tsp salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 8 tbsp butter
  • 1/2 cup chopped chives
  • salt and pepper to finish

Directions

  1. Whisk 1/2 cup milk, 42 g flour & 1/2 cup water over medium heat until stiff paste forms - about 2 minutes.
  2. Whisk yeast into 2 tbsp warm milk until dissolved.
  3. Add sour cream, sugar, salt, 2 eggs, 4 tbsp butter and 625 g flour to tangzhong in stand mixer. Knead with dough hook for 8-10 minutes.
  4. Prove until doubled, 1-1.5 hours.
  5. Roll dough into 12 in square, sprinkle chives and roll up.
  6. Flatten and roll into 16x6 in rectangle and cut into 24 2 in pieces.
  7. Roll into balls and place into buttered 13x9 in pan. Cover and proof for 45-60 minutes until doubled.
  8. Egg wash rolls, sprinkle with salt and pepper and bake 25-30 minutes at 375.
  9. Brush tops with 2 tbsp butter immediately out of oven. Let cool in pan for 5 minutes, then remove to wire rack.
